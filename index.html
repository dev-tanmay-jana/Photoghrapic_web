Photoghraphy:

The word Photography literally means 'drawing with light', which derives from the Greek photo, meaning light and graph, meaning to draw. Photography is the process of recording an image a photograph on lightsensitive film or, in the case of digital photography, via a digital electronic or magnetic memory.The camera is the image-forming device, and a photographic plate, photographic film or a silicon electronic image sensor is the capture medium. The respective recording medium can be the plate or film itself, or a digital magnetic or electronic memory. Photographers control the camera and lens to "expose" the light recording material to the required amount of light to form a "latent image" (on plate or film) or RAW file (in digital cameras) which, after appropriate processing, is converted to a usable image.

Vedioghraphy:

Videography is the process of capturing moving images electronically and then editing and post-producing them. It can include recording on videotape, digital cameras, or streaming media. Videographers typically work on smaller projects, such as weddings, concerts, or live events, and often work as a one-person team or small crew.Videography is the art and science of capturing moving images. It involves using cameras and other equipment to record video footage, and then editing and producing that footage into a final video product. Videographers use a variety of techniques to create visually appealing and engaging videos.

Cinamatoghraphy :

Cinematography is the art of capturing moving images, specifically for films and television. It involves everything from lighting and camera angles to choosing the right lenses and film stock. A cinematographer, also known as a director of photography, is responsible for the visual style of a production.Cinematography is the art and craft of capturing moving images using a film or digital camera. In narrative films and television shows, cinematography tells a visual story through light, color, choice of camera lens, the way the camera moves through the scene and what is included or focused on in the frame.

Traditional :

Traditional videography refers to the classic approach to capturing video footage, prioritizing documentation over artistic expression. It involves using standard video cameras to record events in a straightforward manner, often with a focus on capturing every moment chronologically. Traditional videography captures the ceremony, toasts, and other formalities from start to finish. It's usually filmed with a bulky camcorder in a static position, and the videographer doesn't get involved with the couple.
